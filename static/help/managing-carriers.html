Managing Carriers

Understand carrier profiles, upload documents, review authority, and track insurance from one place.

4–7 minutes Beginner

Understanding Carrier Profiles

Once a carrier has been added to My Carriers, the carrier profile becomes the main place to review status, agreements, insurance, and documents. It is designed to give you a complete operational view of each carrier without jumping between screens.

Step 1 — Open the carrier profile
  • Open any carrier from search results or from My Carriers.
  • The top section shows key status details such as operating status, authority, and safety information.
  • Use this page as the main record for reviewing and managing that carrier.
Step 2 — Review the main profile sections
  • The profile includes agreements, equipment summary, safety data, operations and authority details, insurance coverages, and documents.
  • Status badges at the top make it easy to quickly confirm whether a carrier is active and authorized.
  • Jump links help you move between sections without leaving the carrier record.
Tip
If you need to verify the current state of a carrier quickly, start with the badges and summary section at the top of the profile.

Uploading Documents

Carrier Shark keeps carrier-related documents tied to the carrier record. This makes it easy to manage broker-uploaded files and carrier-submitted files in one place.

Step 1 — Review signed agreements
  • Signed agreements appear in the agreements section near the top of the carrier profile.
  • You can open the signed agreement and its signing certificate directly from this section.
  • This gives you a clear record of which agreement was signed and when it was completed.
Step 2 — Review carrier documents
  • The documents section stores files such as W-9s, ACH forms, insurance certificates, and other uploaded documents.
  • Carrier-submitted files and broker-uploaded files stay attached to the carrier record.
  • Use the table to review document type, upload date, and available actions.
Step 3 — Upload your own documents
  • Use Add Document to upload documents directly to the carrier profile.
  • This is useful for broker-side paperwork, updated forms, or files received outside the standard agreement flow.
  • Uploaded documents remain organized under the same carrier for future reference.

Monitoring Authority Changes

Carrier profiles are built to make authority and operating status easy to review. You can quickly confirm whether a carrier is active, authorized, and in good standing directly from the profile.

Step 1 — Review authority badges
  • Authority and operating status appear prominently at the top of the carrier profile.
  • Use these badges to quickly check whether a carrier is active and authorized.
  • Broker, common, and contract authority details are also visible when available.
Step 2 — Use the profile to confirm current status
  • The profile is meant to give you a fast operational view without needing to dig through multiple records.
  • When reviewing a carrier, start here before sending agreements or working from existing paperwork.
  • For alert setup and change notifications, see the Alerts & Monitoring guide.

Insurance Tracking

When insurance information is available, Carrier Shark displays coverage details directly in the carrier profile. This helps you review the carrier’s current coverages and access supporting insurance documents from one place.

Step 1 — Review insurance coverages
  • The insurance section displays available coverage types, policy information, effective dates, and expiration dates.
  • Use the coverage cards to quickly understand what is on file for that carrier.
  • If a certificate is available, you can open it directly from the insurance section.
Step 2 — Cross-check documents and insurance
  • Insurance-related files can also appear in the documents section at the bottom of the profile.
  • This makes it easy to compare coverages shown on the profile with the uploaded certificate or supporting paperwork.
  • Keeping both views together helps reduce back-and-forth when reviewing carrier compliance.
